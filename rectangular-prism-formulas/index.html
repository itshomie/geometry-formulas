Direct answer

What are the main rectangular prism formulas?

The main rectangular prism formulas are volume V=lwh, surface area SA=2(lw+lh+wh), and base area B=lw. Use length, width, and height in the same unit before multiplying or adding face areas.

Short answer

Rectangular Prism formulas at a glance

Volume: V=lwh Surface area: SA=2(lw+lh+wh) Base area: B=lw

Definition

What is a rectangular prism?

A rectangular prism is a 3D solid with six rectangular faces.

Formula explanation

How to use these formulas

Volume is base area times height. Surface area adds the areas of the three unique face pairs and doubles the sum.

Examples

Step-by-step examples

Find volume

  1. Given l = 8 cm, w = 4 cm, h = 3 cm.
  2. Use V=lwh.
  3. V=8×4×3=96cm3.

Find surface area

  1. Given l = 8, w = 4, h = 3.
  2. Use SA=2(lw+lh+wh).
  3. SA=2(32+24+12)=136cm2.

Find base area

  1. Given l = 8 cm and w = 4 cm.
  2. Use B=lw.
  3. B=8×4=32cm2.

Find height from volume

  1. Given V = 120 cm3, l = 10 cm, w = 4 cm.
  2. Use h=V/lw.
  3. h=120/40=3cm.

Real-world box problem

  1. A box is 5 in by 4 in by 9 in.
  2. Use SA=2(lw+lh+wh).
  3. SA=2(20+45+36)=202in2.

Common mistakes

Common mistakes

  • Using only one face area for surface area.
  • Writing volume in square units.
  • Mixing different units before multiplying dimensions.
