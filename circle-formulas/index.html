Direct answer

What are the main circle formulas?

The main circle formulas are area A=πr2, circumference C=2πr or C=πd, diameter d=2r, and radius r=d/2. Use radius for area; if diameter or circumference is given, convert to radius first.

Short answer

Circle formulas at a glance

Area: A=πr2 Circumference: C=2πr=πd Diameter: d=2r

Definition

What is a circle?

A circle is a 2D shape made of all points that are the same distance from a center point.

Formula explanation

How to use these formulas

The radius is the key circle measurement. If you know diameter or circumference, convert it to radius first, then use the area formula.

Examples

Step-by-step examples

Given radius, find area

  1. Given r = 5 cm.
  2. Use A=πr2.
  3. A=π×52=25π≈78.54cm2.

Given diameter, find circumference

  1. Given d = 12 m.
  2. Use C=πd.
  3. C=12π≈37.70m.

Given circumference, find radius

  1. Given C = 31.4 in.
  2. Use r=C/2π.
  3. r=31.4/6.283...≈5in.

Given area, find radius

  1. Given A = 100π cm2.
  2. Use r=A/π​.
  3. r=100π/π​=10cm.

Real-world circle problem

  1. A round table has diameter 4 ft.
  2. Use A=π(d/2)2.
  3. A=π×22=4π≈12.57ft2.

Common mistakes

Common mistakes

  • Using diameter in the area formula without dividing by 2 first.
  • Writing circumference in square units. Circumference uses linear units.
  • Rounding pi too early when the problem asks for an exact answer.

Practice

Practice problems with answers

1. A circle has radius 7 cm. Find its area.

A=π×72=49π≈153.94cm2.

2. A circle has diameter 18 m. Find its circumference.

C=πd=18π≈56.55m.

3. A circle has circumference 20π in. Find its radius.

r=C/2π=20π/2π=10in.

4. A circle has area 64π ft2. Find its diameter.

r=64π/π​=8ft, so d=16ft.

5. A round rug has radius 3 ft. How much floor area does it cover?

A=9π≈28.27ft2.

FAQ

Circle Formulas FAQ

What is the formula for area of a circle?

The area formula is A=πr2, where r is the radius.

What is the circumference formula?

Circumference can be found with C=2πr or C=πd.

How do you find radius from diameter?

Divide the diameter by 2: r=d/2.
