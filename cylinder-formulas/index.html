Direct answer

What are the main cylinder formulas?

The main cylinder formulas are volume V=πr2h, lateral surface area LSA=2πrh, total surface area TSA=2πr(r+h), and base area B=πr2. Use radius and height, not diameter, in these formulas.

Short answer

Cylinder formulas at a glance

Volume: V=πr2h Lateral surface area: LSA=2πrh Total surface area: TSA=2πr(r+h)

Definition

What is a cylinder?

A cylinder is a 3D solid with two congruent circular bases connected by a curved surface.

Formula explanation

How to use these formulas

The cylinder volume formula is base area times height. Surface area includes the curved side plus two circular bases.

Examples

Step-by-step examples

Find volume

  1. Given r = 3 cm and h = 10 cm.
  2. Use V=πr2h.
  3. V=90π≈282.74cm3.

Find lateral surface area

  1. Given r = 3 cm and h = 10 cm.
  2. Use LSA=2πrh.
  3. LSA=60π≈188.50cm2.

Find total surface area

  1. Given r = 3 cm and h = 10 cm.
  2. Use TSA=2πr(r+h).
  3. TSA=78π≈245.04cm2.

Find height from volume

  1. Given V = 144π cm3 and r = 6 cm.
  2. Use h=V/(πr2).
  3. h=144π/36π=4cm.

Real-world cylinder problem

  1. A can has r = 4 cm and h = 12 cm.
  2. Use V=πr2h.
  3. V=192π≈603.19cm3.

Common mistakes

Common mistakes

  • Using diameter as radius.
  • Forgetting the two circular bases in total surface area.
  • Writing volume in square units instead of cubic units.
