Direct answer

What are geometry formulas?

Geometry formulas are equations used to calculate measurements such as area, perimeter, circumference, volume, and surface area. Common examples include circle area A=πr2, rectangle area A=lw, cylinder volume V=πr2h, and sphere surface area SA=4πr2.

Basic Geometry Formulas

Core measurements

Most geometry problems ask for one of five measurements: area, perimeter, circumference, volume, or surface area.

MeasurementSymbolMeaning
AreaASpace inside a 2D shape, measured in square units

CircumferenceCDistance around a circle
VolumeVSpace inside a 3D solid, measured in cubic units

Geometry Formula Examples

Worked examples

Area of a circle

  1. Given r = 5 cm.
  2. Use A=πr2.
  3. A=π×52=25π≈78.54cm2.

Volume of a cylinder

  1. Given r = 3 m and h = 10 m.
  2. Use V=πr2h.
  3. V=π×32×10=90π≈282.74m3.

Surface area of a sphere

  1. Given r = 6 in.
  2. Use SA=4πr2.
  3. SA=4π×62=144π≈452.39in2.

FAQ

Geometry formulas FAQ

What are the most important geometry formulas to memorize?

Start with area, perimeter, circumference, volume, and surface area formulas for square, rectangle, triangle, circle, cylinder, cone, and sphere.

Is this page a geometry formulas sheet?

Yes. It is designed as a geometry formulas sheet for quick reference, with tables, diagrams, examples, and calculators.

What is the difference between perimeter and area?

Perimeter measures distance around a 2D shape. Area measures the amount of flat space inside the shape.

What is the difference between volume and surface area?

Volume measures the space inside a 3D object. Surface area measures the total area of its outside faces or curved surfaces.
