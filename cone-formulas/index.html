Direct answer

What are the main cone formulas?

The main cone formulas are volume V=31​πr2h, lateral surface area LSA=πrl, total surface area TSA=πr(r+l), and slant height l=r2+h2​. Surface area uses slant height, not vertical height.

Definition

What is a cone?

A cone is a 3D solid with one circular base and one vertex connected by a curved side.

Formula explanation

How to use these formulas

A cone has one third the volume of a cylinder with the same base and height. Surface area needs slant height, not vertical height.

Examples

Step-by-step examples

Find slant height

  1. Given r = 4 cm and h = 9 cm.
  2. Use l=r2+h2​.
  3. l=97​≈9.85cm.

Find volume

  1. Given r = 4 cm and h = 9 cm.
  2. Use V=31​πr2h.
  3. V=48π≈150.80cm3.

Find total surface area

  1. Given r = 4 cm and l ≈ 9.85 cm.
  2. Use TSA=πr(r+l).
  3. TSA≈π×4(4+9.85)≈174.05cm2.

Find height from volume

  1. Given V = 48π cm3 and r = 4 cm.
  2. Use h=3V/(πr2).
  3. h=3(48π)/16π=9cm.

Find lateral surface area

  1. Given r = 5 cm and l = 13 cm.
  2. Use LSA=πrl.
  3. LSA=65π≈204.20cm2.

FAQ

Cone Formulas FAQ

What is the cone volume formula?

The formula is V=31​πr2h.

What is slant height?

Slant height is the distance from the cone vertex to a point on the edge of the circular base.

What is the total surface area of a cone?

The formula is TSA=πr(r+l).
