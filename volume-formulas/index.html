Direct answer

What is a volume formula?

Volume formulas calculate the space inside a 3D solid. Common examples are cube volume V=s3, rectangular prism volume V=lwh, cylinder volume V=πr2h, cone volume V=31​πr2h, and sphere volume V=34​πr3.

Examples

Step-by-step examples

Rectangular prism volume

  1. Given l = 8 cm, w = 4 cm, h = 3 cm.
  2. Use V=lwh.
  3. V=8×4×3=96cm3.

Cylinder volume

  1. Given r = 2 m and h = 9 m.
  2. Use V=πr2h.
  3. V=36π≈113.10m3.

Sphere volume

  1. Given r = 5 in.
  2. Use V=34​πr3.
  3. V=500/3π≈523.60in3.

FAQ

Volume Formulas FAQ

What is the easiest volume formula?

For prisms, volume is usually base area times height, or V=Bh.

Why does a cone use one third?

A cone with the same base and height as a cylinder has one third of the cylinder volume.

Do volume answers need cubic units?

Yes. Volume is written in cubic units, such as cm3 or cubic meters.
